Device-by-device teardown guides. Each covers what is typically inside, what parts are worth pulling, and what hazards to account for before opening.

## Low risk

[Battery Devices](01-battery-devices.html) — Toys, remotes, hand tools, and other small low-voltage gadgets. Easy first donors with simple boards.

[Routers and Modems](03-routers-modems.html) — Low-voltage DC internally; powered by an external adapter. Good yields of connectors, caps, and switching regulators.

[Printers](04-printers.html) — Low-voltage logic boards with motors, belts, sensors, and stepper drivers. Laser printer fusers run at mains voltage.

[Desktop Computers](05-desktop-computers.html) — Wide variety of parts once the PSU is removed and set aside. Treat the PSU as a separate donor.

[Laptops](06-laptops.html) — Remove the battery first. Dense boards with useful DC-DC converters, connectors, and small motors.

[LED Bulbs](09-led-bulbs.html) — Small driver boards with useful resistors and capacitors; LED strings vary in quality.

## Moderate hazard

[Wall Chargers](02-wall-chargers.html) — Internal caps may retain charge after unplugging. Useful switching regulators and rectifier diodes.

## Significant hazard

[ATX Power Supplies](07-atx-power-supplies.html) — Large input caps hold 300 V or more for several minutes after unplugging. Discharge before reaching in.

[Audio Equipment](08-audio-equipment.html) — Mains-powered; supply caps hold charge. Valve amplifiers are a different category entirely — treat as lethal.

[Solar Electric Fence Chargers](13-solar-fence-chargers.html) — Output caps hold 2–10 kV. Must be fully discharged before touching any internal point.

## Lethal danger

[Microwave Ovens](10-microwave-ovens.html) — The HV capacitor holds a lethal charge for hours or days after unplugging. Do not open unless you know how to discharge it safely.

[CRT Monitors](11-crt-monitors.html) — The anode can hold 25,000 V or more for weeks. Requires specific discharge procedure before any contact.

[UPS Units](12-ups-units.html) — Batteries remain live regardless of mains connection and can deliver very high short-circuit current.
