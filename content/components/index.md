Reference pages for common salvaged parts. Each page covers how to identify the component, what to measure, and how to decide whether it is worth keeping.

## Passive components

[Resistors](01-resistors.html) — The most common part on any board. Covers colour codes, SMD markings, shunts, fusibles, and when a part that reads correctly has still drifted.

[Capacitors](02-capacitors.html) — Electrolytics, ceramics, and films. How to read ratings, spot failures, and test ESR and capacitance before adding to stock.

[Inductors and Transformers](07-inductors-transformers.html) — Power inductors, ferrite chokes, and small mains transformers. Winding ratio measurement, continuity checks, and core types.

## Semiconductors

[Diodes](03-diodes.html) — Rectifier, Schottky, Zener, and signal diodes. How to identify from markings, measure forward voltage, and confirm breakdown for Zeners.

[BJT Transistors](04-transistors-bjt.html) — NPN and PNP junction transistors. Identifying package and type, measuring hFE and junction voltages, and spotting leaky parts.

[MOSFETs](05-mosfets.html) — N- and P-channel enhancement-mode devices. Gate threshold, Rds(on) check, and how to distinguish logic-level from standard-voltage parts.

[Voltage Regulators](06-voltage-regulators.html) — Linear regulators (78xx, LDO) and switching ICs. Identifying output voltage, dropout, and whether the part has any life left.

## Electromechanical

[Relays](08-relays.html) — Coil voltage, contact rating, pull-in and drop-out currents, and the contact resistance check that separates usable relays from scrap.

[Motors](09-motors.html) — DC brush, stepper, and small AC motors. Winding resistance, mechanical condition, and what each type is actually useful for.

[Connectors and Switches](10-connectors-switches.html) — Pin headers, barrel jacks, USB ports, and tactile switches. Identifying pitch, current rating, and whether contacts are still serviceable.
